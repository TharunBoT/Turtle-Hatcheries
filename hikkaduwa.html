Hikkaduwa!

Hikkaduwa is a coastal town in Galle district of Sri Lanka. It is known for its beaches and corals. Hikkaduwa is a major tourist attraction in Sri Lanka and is famous for its long sandy beaches, good waves for surfers, reefs for snorkelers and divers, plus sand-side hotels, restuarants and bars

Things to do in Hikkaduwa

1.Hikkaduwa Beach!

Hikkaduwa beach is located in the Southwestern part of Sri Lanka. It is one of the most beatiful beaches in Sri Lanka that characterized by palm trees lining the shore, clear blue waters with the backdrop of sea life including, warm-shelled turtles, beatiful corals and different kind of fishes. It is a major tourist attraction in Sri Lanka and is known for its beaches and corals.

2.Community Tsunami Museum

The Community Tsunami Museum is located in north Hikkaduwa in Sri Lanka. The museum aimed to educate communities on tsunami and preserving the memories of the 2004 tsunami. This museum will take you on a hearth touching journey.

3.Tsunami Honganji Vihara

Tsunami Honganji Vihara is a statue of Buddha in the Peraliya-Hikkaduwa region. It was a gift from Japan to remember over 40,000 Sri Lankan victims of the Sumatran-Andaman tsunami. The statue is located right on the coast north of Hikkaduwa and shows a standing Buddha facing the waves with his hands in the "Abhaya mudra"

4.Hikkaduwa Coral Garden

Hikkaduwa Coral Garden is situated just off the coast of Hikkaduwa Beach in Sri Lanka. The coral reefs here are located in shallow waters, making it an ideal spot for both beginners and experienced snorkelers. The beach reefs at Hikkaduwa are the most diverse and accessible. Coral Garden is a typical coral reef with a shallow ridge, in a protected lagoon. Up to about 200 meters from the coast, the sea is very shallow .

Turtle Hatchery Hikkaduwa

If you're in Hikkaduwa, the sea turtle hatchery is a lovely spot to visit. If you love animals, seeing the turtles in person is a great experience. It's a family-run conservation hatchery, A guy by the name of Ariyapala established it and passed it on to his daughter, who tragically died in the tsunami of 2004, Ariyapala's son took over and rebuilt the hatchery with assistance from abroad.


Now its a home for many sea turtles that need help to protect their life from many threats...

Conservation Projects

The hatchery and rescue facility currently cares for five different species of turtles. These species include the Green Turtle, Hawksbill Turtle, Leatherback Turtle, and Olive Ridley Turtle. Modern water tanks are included in the facilities at the sea turtle hatchery and rescue center, which also cares for adult turtle species that have been pulled from the water after suffering injuries. The place where the turtles lay their eggs, which has distinct small mounds of sand piled neatly between one another, is one of the more odd sights in the center. Each of these mounds is closely observed to determine when the turtle eggs will hatch.
