Bentota Turtle Hatchery!

Sri Lanka is one of the world's major biodiversity hotspots, and the preservation of the island's wildlife is a top priority. By supporting Sri Lanka's turtles' survival, the numerous turtle hatcheries that dot the western coastline attempt to solve the problem.

The eggs are kept secure in the sand until they break out of their shells, at which point they are transported and put into tanks where they will spend the first few days of their life. This increases their chances of surviving by giving them a chance to get stronger before being returned to the perils of the water. In this way, a number of turtle species are preserved.
